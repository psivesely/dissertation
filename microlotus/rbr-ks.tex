
\parhead{PIOR composition formulation}
We prove RBR-KS compositionally using the claim-flow DAG in \cref{fig:ringmul-pior-dag}. Each box is a PIOR. For any nonterminal box, its local source relation is the conjunction of the incoming arrow labels, and its local target relation is the conjunction of the outgoing arrow labels, with the relevant oracle handles included in the local instance. Terminal verifier-side checks that are consumed inside a box (rather than forwarded) are written in the box itself and contribute zero additional knowledge error.

\parhead{Round-by-round knowledge soundness}

\begin{theorem}[RBR knowledge soundness of $\Pi_{\mathsf{RingMul}}$]
\label{thm:rbr}
Viewed as a PIOR $\Pi_{\mathsf{RingMul}}: \relation_{\mathsf{RM}} \to \relation_{\mathsf{eval}}$, the protocol has round-by-round knowledge soundness with error vector
\[
\bigl(
\kappa_{\alpha,\alpha'},\;
\underbrace{2/|\extensionfield|,\dotsc,2/|\extensionfield|}_{\mu\text{ copies}},\;
\underbrace{3/|\extensionfield|,\dotsc,3/|\extensionfield|}_{\nu\text{ copies}},\;
\kappa_{\xi},\;
\underbrace{2/|\extensionfield|,\dotsc,2/|\extensionfield|}_{\nu\text{ copies}}
\bigr),
\]
where $\kappa_{\alpha,\alpha'} \le (\tau+\nu)/|\extensionfield|$ and $\kappa_{\xi}\le 2/|\extensionfield|$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
We prove the four boxes in \cref{fig:ringmul-pior-dag} separately and concatenate the resulting error vectors.

For the two nonsumcheck PIORs, fix one deterministic extractor: query $\oracle{\tilde A},\oracle{\tilde s},\oracle{\tilde t}$ on the Boolean hypercubes and recover the unique triple $(A,s,t)$ encoded by the oracles. For the two sumcheck PIORs, we use the standard RBR-KS bound for sumcheck: fixing a variable of degree $d$ contributes error $d/|\extensionfield|$ in that round. Here $\Pi_{\mathsf{sc1}}$ has degree $2$ in each $J$-round and degree $3$ in each $K$-round, while $\Pi_{\mathsf{sc2}}$ has degree $2$ in every round.

\parhead{$\Pi_{\alpha,\alpha'}$ \textnormal{(1 round, error $\kappa_{\alpha,\alpha'}$)}}
The knowledge state moves from $0$ to $1$ at the verifier challenge $(\alpha,\alpha')$ exactly when
\[
t_C(\alpha,\alpha')
=
\sum_{k\in\bits^\nu}\meq(k,\alpha')\sum_{j\in\bits^\mu} A_C(\alpha,j,k)\,s_C(j,k)
\]
even though the multilinear identity
\[
t_C(I,K)
=
\sum_{k\in\bits^\nu}\meq(k,K)\sum_{j\in\bits^\mu} A_C(I,j,k)\,s_C(j,k)
\]
fails. Since both sides are multilinear in $(I,K)$ and disagree on some Boolean point whenever $As\neq t$, this happens with probability at most $(\tau+\nu)/|\extensionfield|$. When it happens, the prover can choose $e_t$ to satisfy both outgoing claims. Therefore $\kappa_{\alpha,\alpha'}\le (\tau+\nu)/|\extensionfield|$.

\parhead{$\Pi_{\xi}$ \textnormal{(1 round, error $\kappa_{\xi}$)}}
Define
\[
Q(Z) := (e_A - A_C(\alpha,\beta,\gamma)) + Z(e_s - s_C(\beta,\gamma)) + Z^2(e_t - t_C(\alpha,\alpha')).
\]
A $0 \to 1$ transition at the verifier challenge $\xi$ can occur only if the outgoing batched check holds, that is, only if $Q(\xi)=0$, while $Q$ is not the zero polynomial.

If $e_t \neq t_C(\alpha,\alpha')$, then the $Z^2$ coefficient is nonzero, so $Q$ is not the zero polynomial. Otherwise $e_t = t_C(\alpha,\alpha')$, and since by hypothesis the knowledge state is $0$, the incoming product claim must fail: $\sigma' \neq A_C(\alpha,\beta,\gamma)s_C(\beta,\gamma)\meq(\gamma,\alpha')$.
But transitioning to $1$ requires $\sigma' = e_A e_s \meq(\gamma,\alpha')$.
If $\meq(\gamma,\alpha')=0$, these two conditions are incompatible, so any $0 \to 1$ transition must satisfy $\meq(\gamma,\alpha') \neq 0$. In that case,
$e_A e_s \neq A_C(\alpha,\beta,\gamma)s_C(\beta,\gamma)$,
so at least one of $e_A \neq A_C(\alpha,\beta,\gamma)$ or $e_s \neq s_C(\beta,\gamma)$ holds. Thus the constant or linear coefficient of $Q$ is nonzero, and again $Q$ is not the zero polynomial.

Therefore every $0 \to 1$ transition forces a nonzero quadratic $Q$ to vanish at $Z=\xi$, which happens with probability at most $2/|\extensionfield|$. Hence $\kappa_{\xi} \le 2/|\extensionfield|$.
\end{proof}

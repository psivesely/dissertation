\section{PIOPs over fields for cyclotomic ring arithmetic}
\label{sec:ring-piop}

We first give the core matrix-vector PIOR in~\cref{sec:matrix-vector-pior}, which reduces proving $As \equiv t$ over $\ring_p$ to a small number of evaluation claims on the coefficient-domain oracles. Its prover and verifier complexity are $O(NMn)$ and $O(n + \log NM)$, and its proof size is $O(\log Mn)$. We show completeness, round-by-round knowledge soundness in~\cref{sec:ring-pior-ks}. Next, \cref{sec:holographic-piop} presents a holographic specialization for the public $A$ setting, where its commitment is output by an indexer. We show this allows a significantly simplified and more efficient protocol. Finally, \cref{sec:reducing-commitments}, using a ring-Plonkish SpeedySpartan running example, describes how a chain of PIORs can reduce verification to two query claims: one the MLE of the prover's witness and the other on an indexed oracle, wrapping up with a couple final optimizations in the PCS domain.

\subsection{A PIOR for cyclotomic matrix-vector multiplication}
\label{sec:matrix-vector-pior}

Fix a prime~$p$, $n = 2^\nu$ with $p \equiv 1 \pmod{2n}$, $N = 2^\tau$, $M = 2^\mu$, and let $\mathcal{R}_p = \ZZ_p[X]/(X^n+1)$.

\begin{definition}
\label{def:source-rel}
The \emph{ring multiplication relation} $\relation_{\mathsf{RM}}$ consists of triples $(\index, \instance, \witness)$ where:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item Index: $\index = (p, \nu, \tau, \mu)$.
  \item Instance: $\instance = \bigl(\oracle{\tilde{A}},\, \oracle{\tilde{s}},\, \oracle{\tilde{t}}\bigr)$, three multilinear polynomial oracles over~$\field_p$ of arities $\tau{+}\mu{+}\nu$, $\mu{+}\nu$, and $\tau{+}\nu$ respectively.
  \item Witness: $\witness = (A,s,t) \in \mathcal{R}_p^{N \times M} \times \mathcal{R}_p^M \times \mathcal{R}_p^N$.
  \item Membership: $As = t$ in~$\mathcal{R}_p^N$, and the oracles are the multilinear extensions of the coefficient representations of~$A$, $s$, $t$.
\end{itemize}
\end{definition}

The target relation is the polynomial evaluation relation $\relation_{\mathsf{eval}}$, whose triples $(\index', \instance', \witness')$ consist of a list of evaluation claims $\instance' = \{(\oracle{f_j}, z_j, v_j)\}_{j=1}^m$ on polynomial oracles, with membership requiring $f_j(z_j) = v_j$ for all~$j$. The witness~$\witness'$ is unconstrained: membership depends only on the oracles and the claimed evaluations.

Let $\omega \in \field_p$ be a primitive $2n$-th root of unity and let $\tilde{C} \in \field_p^{\multilin}[\bm{K},\bm{L}]$ denote the multilinear extension of the CRT matrix $C = \crtnosubscript_{n,p}$,
\[
    C_{k,\ell} = \omega^{(2k+1)\ell}, \qquad k,\ell \in \{0,1\}^\nu,
\]
which maps coefficient vectors to evaluations at the roots of $X^n + 1$. Define virtual polynomials
\[
    \begin{array}{ll}
    A_C(i,j,k) &:= \sum_{\ell \in \bits^\nu} \tilde{C}(k,\ell)\cdot\tilde{A}(i,j,\ell), \\
    s_C(j,k) &:= \sum_{\ell \in \bits^\nu} \tilde{C}(k,\ell)\cdot\tilde{s}(j,\ell), \\
    t_C(i,k) &:= \sum_{\ell \in \bits^\nu} \tilde{C}(k,\ell)\cdot\tilde{t}(i,\ell).
    \end{array}
\]
When $t \equiv A s \mod p$, the CRT transform gives the pointwise relation $t_C(i,k) = \sum_{j \in \bits^\mu} A_{C}(i,j,k)\cdot s_{C}(j,k)$ for all $(i,k) \in \bits^{\tau+\nu}$. Since $A_C$ and $s_C$ are individually multilinear in $\bm{K}$, the product $\sum_j A_C \cdot s_C$ has individual degree~$2$ in $\bm{K}$; the sumcheck therefore includes an $\meq(\bm{K},\alpha')$ kernel to project onto the multilinear extension in $\bm{K}$ and ensure completeness.

\begin{figure}[H]
\caption{Protocol $\Pi_{\mathsf{RingMul}}=(\prover, \verifier)$: a PIOR for checking matrix-vector multiplication in a cyclotomic ring $\ring_p = \ZZ_p[X]/(X^{2^\nu}+1)$ over $\field_p$}
\label{prot:ringmul}
\begin{algorithm}[H]
\textbf{Input:} The verifier receives oracles $\oracle{\tilde{A}} \in \field_{p}^{\multilin}[\bm{I},\bm{J},\bm{K}]$, $\oracle{\tilde{s}} \in \field_{p}^{\multilin}[\bm{J},\bm{K}],$ and $\oracle{\tilde{t}} \in \field_{p}^{\multilin}[\bm{I},\bm{K}]$. The prover receives matrix $A \in \ring_{p}^{N\times M}$ and vectors $s \in \ring_{p}^{M}$, $t \in \ring_{p}^{N}$ such that
\[
    \tilde{A}(i,j,k) = \coeff_k(A_{i,j}), \qquad
    \tilde{s}(j,k) = \coeff_k(s_j), \qquad
    \tilde{t}(i,k) = \coeff_k(t_i)
\]
holds for $i \in \bits^\tau, j \in \bits^\mu, k \in \bits^\nu$, where $\tau = \log N$, $\mu = \log M$, $\nu = \log n$, and $n = 2^\nu$ is the degree of the cyclotomic polynomial.

\begin{algorithmic}[1]
    \STATE The verifier samples $\alpha \randpick \extensionfield^{\tau}$, $\alpha' \randpick \extensionfield^{\nu}$.

    \STATE The prover sends $e_t$ claimed to equal $t_C(\alpha, \alpha')$.

    \STATE $\prover$ and $\verifier$ engage in a sumcheck for the claim
    \[
        e_t = \sum_{(j,k) \in \bits^{\mu+\nu}} A_C(\alpha, j, k) \cdot s_C(j, k) \cdot \meq(k, \alpha')
    \]
    with challenges $(\beta,\gamma) \randpick \extensionfield^{\mu+\nu}$, reducing to the claim $\sigma' = A_C(\alpha, \beta, \gamma) \cdot s_C(\beta, \gamma) \cdot \meq(\gamma, \alpha')$.

    \STATE The prover sends $e_A$ and $e_s$ claimed to equal $A_C(\alpha, \beta, \gamma)$ and $s_C(\beta, \gamma)$ respectively. The verifier evaluates $\meq(\gamma,\alpha')$ in $O(\nu)$ operations and checks
    \[
        \sigma' \;=\; e_A \cdot e_s \cdot \meq(\gamma, \alpha').
    \]

    \STATE The verifier samples batching challenge $\xi \randpick \extensionfield$.

    \STATE $\prover$ and $\verifier$ engage in a sumcheck for the claim
    \[
        e_A + \xi \cdot e_s + \xi^{2} \cdot e_t  = \sum_{\ell \in \bits^{\nu}} \Big[\tilde{C}(\gamma, \ell) \cdot \big(\tilde{A}(\alpha, \beta, \ell) + \xi \cdot \tilde{s}(\beta, \ell)\big) + \xi^{2} \cdot \tilde{C}(\alpha', \ell) \cdot \tilde{t}(\alpha, \ell)\Big]
    \]
    with folding challenges $\delta \randpick \extensionfield^{\nu}$, reducing to the claim
    \[
        \sigma'' = \tilde{C}(\gamma, \delta) \cdot \big(\tilde{A}(\alpha, \beta, \delta) + \xi \cdot \tilde{s}(\beta, \delta)\big) + \xi^{2} \cdot \tilde{C}(\alpha', \delta) \cdot \tilde{t}(\alpha, \delta).
    \]

    \STATE The verifier evaluates $\tilde{C}(\gamma, \delta)$ and $\tilde{C}(\alpha', \delta)$ in $O(n)$ field operations (\cref{lem:m-eval}), queries $e'_A \gets \oracle{\tilde{A}(\alpha, \beta, \delta)}$, $e'_s \gets \oracle{\tilde{s}(\beta, \delta)}$, $e'_t \gets \oracle{\tilde{t}(\alpha, \delta)}$, and checks
    \[
        \sigma'' = \tilde{C}(\gamma, \delta) \cdot \big(e'_A + \xi \cdot e'_s\big) + \xi^{2} \cdot \tilde{C}(\alpha', \delta) \cdot e'_t.
    \]
\end{algorithmic}
\end{algorithm}
\end{figure}

\subsection{A holographic variant}
\label{sec:holographic-piop}

We describe a holographic variant that corresponds to how this protocol would be used as a subroutine in a more general PIOP for NP (e.g., a CCS or R1CS proof system over cyclotomic rings).

In the main protocol (\cref{prot:ringmul}), Sumcheck~1 includes $\nu$ rounds for the $\bm{K}$-variables at degree~$3$, because the product $A_C \cdot s_C$ is degree~$2$ in $\bm{K}$ and the $\meq(\bm{K},\alpha')$ kernel adds another factor. Moreover, Sumcheck~2 must evaluate $\tilde{C}$ at two CRT points ($\gamma$ for $A_C,s_C$ and $\alpha'$ for $t_C$). Both costs stem from the fact that the prover constructs $A_C$ and $s_C$ as virtual polynomials $\sum_\ell \tilde{C}(k,\ell)\tilde{A}(\cdot)$ and $\sum_\ell \tilde{C}(k,\ell)\tilde{s}(\cdot)$, making $k$ a free variable. In the holographic setting, these extra rounds and the second sumcheck can be eliminated entirely.

We make two assumptions:
\begin{enumerate}[nosep]
    \item $A$ is public and the indexer supplies the CRT-domain oracle $\oracle{A_C} \in \extensionfield^{\multilin}[\bm{I},\bm{J},\bm{K}]$ directly.
    \item $t_C$ is a virtual polynomial whose evaluations are determined by the outer protocol: the prover sends $e_t$ claimed to equal $t_C(\alpha, \alpha')$, and the verifier can check this claim without a CRT consistency sumcheck for~$t$.
\end{enumerate}
The second assumption covers the case where $t$ is public (including the common $t = 0$ SIS setting, where $e_t = 0$ is trivially verified), and also the case where $t_C$ arises from the constraint structure of an outer proof system (see \cref{rem:r1cs-analogy}).

Under these assumptions, the only committed polynomial requiring CRT consistency verification is $\tilde{s}$. Because $A_C$ is now an oracle rather than a virtual polynomial, the verifier can evaluate it directly at the fixed CRT point $\alpha'$: the quantity $A_C(\alpha, j, \alpha')$ depends on $\bm{J}$ alone, and there is no need to sum over $\bm{K}$ with an $\meq$ kernel. This eliminates the degree-$3$ $\bm{K}$-variable rounds from Sumcheck~1. In their place, we inline the CRT expansion $s_C(j, \alpha') = \sum_{\ell \in \bits^\nu} \tilde{C}(\alpha', \ell) \cdot \tilde{s}(j, \ell)$ directly into the sumcheck, yielding degree-$2$ rounds for the coefficient variable $\bm{L}$. Since $A_C$ is an oracle and $t_C$ is externally verified, Sumcheck~2 is eliminated as well, along with the intermediate evaluation claims $e_A, e_s$ and their multiplication check. The merged claim is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:merged-sumcheck}
    e_t \;=\; \sum_{(j,\, \ell) \in \bits^{\mu + \nu}} A_C(\alpha, j, \alpha') \cdot \tilde{C}(\alpha', \ell) \cdot \tilde{s}(j, \ell).
\end{equation}
The virtual polynomial is a product of three multilinears. In each $\bm{J}$-variable round, $A_C(\alpha, \cdot, \alpha')$ and $\tilde{s}$ depend on the current variable while $\tilde{C}(\alpha', \cdot)$ does not; in each $\bm{L}$-variable round, $\tilde{C}(\alpha', \cdot)$ and $\tilde{s}$ depend on the current variable while $A_C$ does not. The round polynomial therefore has degree~2 throughout, and the prover sends $2(\mu + \nu)$ field elements. Compared with the main protocol's $2\mu + 5\nu + 3$ elements (which includes the degree-$3$ rounds in $\bm{K}$, the explicit evaluation claims $e_A$ and $e_s$, and the full Sumcheck~2), this saves $3\nu + 2$ field elements and $\nu+1$ rounds.

After $\mu + \nu$ rounds with challenges $(\beta, \gamma) \randpick \extensionfield^{\mu + \nu}$, the verifier checks the reduced claim
\[
    \sigma \;=\; A_C(\alpha, \beta, \alpha') \cdot \tilde{C}(\alpha', \gamma) \cdot \tilde{s}(\beta, \gamma)
\]
by querying $A_C(\alpha, \beta, \alpha')$ from the indexer oracle, evaluating $\tilde{C}(\alpha', \gamma)$ in $O(n)$ operations (\cref{lem:m-eval}), and querying $e'_s \gets \oracle{\tilde{s}(\beta, \gamma)}$. The output is a single evaluation claim $\tilde{s}(\beta, \gamma) = e'_s$ on the committed oracle. No $\meq$ kernel, evaluation claims $e_A$ or $e_s$, multiplication check, batching challenge, or second sumcheck is needed.

\begin{remark}[Analogy with R1CS second sumcheck]\label{rem:r1cs-analogy}
    The merged sumcheck~\eqref{eq:merged-sumcheck} is the cyclotomic-ring analogue of the ``memory-checking'' or ``oracle-evaluation'' sumcheck that appears in holographic proof systems for R1CS (e.g., Spartan~\cite{spartan}). In a Spartan-style R1CS proof, the witness is $z$ with committed oracle $\oracle{\tilde{z}}$, and the constraint matrices $M \in \{A, B, C\}$ are public with indexed oracles $\oracle{\tilde{M}}$. The first sumcheck verifies $0 = \sum_x \meq(\tau, x) \cdot [z_A(x) \cdot z_B(x) - z_C(x)]$ where $z_M(x) := \sum_y \tilde{M}(x, y) \cdot \tilde{z}(y)$ are virtual polynomials, producing challenges $r$ and claimed evaluations $e_A = z_A(r)$, $e_B = z_B(r)$, $e_C = z_C(r)$. The second sumcheck then verifies these claims via the batched sum
    \[
        e_A + \rho \, e_B + \rho^2 e_C \;=\; \sum_{y} \bigl[\tilde{A}(r, y) + \rho\, \tilde{B}(r, y) + \rho^2\, \tilde{C}(r, y)\bigr] \cdot \tilde{z}(y).
    \]
    The structural correspondence is: $\tilde{s}$ plays the role of $\tilde{z}$ (the committed oracle), $\tilde{C}(\alpha', \cdot)$ plays the role of $\tilde{M}(r, \cdot)$ (a verifier-computable weight function), and the indexed CRT-domain polynomial $A_C(\alpha, \cdot, \alpha')$ plays the role of the constraint-selecting weight in the first sumcheck.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Reducing commitment size and evaluations}
\label{sec:reducing-commitments}

After sumchecks, the verifier holds evaluation claims on committed (prover) and indexed (preprocessed) oracles. Each oracle naively requires its own Merkle tree and PCS opening. We describe two standard PIOR optimizations---selector-polynomial merging and multi-point reduction---that consolidate these claims, and illustrate them with a concrete example. We then describe two ways to shorten the proofs of these consolidated claims: the ring-switch packing from~\cite{EC:DiaPos25}, and technique of sumcheck challenge sharing with interleaved Merkle paths from Basefold~\cite{basefold}.

\paragraph{Running example: SpeedySpartan for Ring Arithmetic.}
\label{sec:speedyspartan}
Consider a SpeedySpartan-style protocol~\cite{speedyspartan} proving arithmetic circuit satisfiability over $\ring_p$. The indexed (preprocessed) oracles are three wiring matrices $\tilde{W}_a, \tilde{W}_b, \tilde{W}_c \in \extensionfield^{\multilin}[\bm{X}, \bm{J}, \bm{L}]$ of dimension $m + \mu + \nu$, and a gate-type selector $\tilde{q}_M \in \extensionfield^{\multilin}[\bm{X}]$ of dimension $m$. The witness oracle $\oracle{\tilde{s}} \in \extensionfield^{\multilin}[\bm{J}, \bm{L}]$ of dimension $\mu + \nu$ is committed by the prover. After an outer (constraint) sumcheck and an inner (memory-checking) sumcheck with challenges $r \in \extensionfield^m$ and $(\beta, \gamma) \in \extensionfield^{\mu + \nu}$---where the inner sumcheck uses the CRT-inlining of \cref{sec:holographic-piop}---the verifier holds five evaluation claims:
\begin{alignat*}{2}
    &\tilde{W}_w(r, \beta, \gamma) = e'_w \;\text{ for } w \in \{a,b,c\}, \qquad &&\tilde{q}_M(r) = e'_q, \\
    &\tilde{s}(\beta, \gamma) = e'_s. &&
\end{alignat*}
The first four oracles are output by the indexer, while $\tilde{s}$ is prover-committed.

\paragraph{SelectorMerge.}
The indexer merges the four indexed oracles into a single oracle $h \in \extensionfield^{\multilin}[B_1, B_2, \bm{X}, \bm{J}, \bm{L}]$ of dimension $2 + m + \mu + \nu$ using two selector bits: $h(0,0,\cdot) = \tilde{W}_a$, $h(0,1,\cdot) = \tilde{W}_b$, $h(1,0,\cdot) = \tilde{W}_c$, and $h(1,1,x,j,\ell) = \tilde{q}_{M,\mathrm{pad}}(x)$, padded to be constant in $(\bm{J}, \bm{L})$. Setting the padded coordinates of $\tilde{q}_M$'s evaluation point to $(\beta, \gamma)$ aligns all four claims to a common data point $(r, \beta, \gamma)$, with evaluation points differing only in the two selector bits:
\begin{alignat*}{2}
    &h(0, 0, r, \beta, \gamma) = e'_a, \quad &&h(0, 1, r, \beta, \gamma) = e'_b, \\
    &h(1, 0, r, \beta, \gamma) = e'_c, \quad &&h(1, 1, r, \beta, \gamma) = e'_q.
\end{alignat*}

\paragraph{MultiPoint.}
The multi-point-to-single-point PIOR~\cite{BCHO22} reduces multiple evaluation claims on one polynomial to a single claim. Given $\ell$ claims $f(z_1) = v_1, \ldots, f(z_\ell) = v_\ell$, the key observation is that whenever the claims cover the full Boolean hypercube $\bits^c$ of a set of $c$ shared coordinates---with all other coordinates identical---the multilinear extension in those $c$ variables is completely determined by the $2^c$ known values, and no prover messages are needed. 

In our setting, $c = 2$ and the four claims cover all of $\bits^2$. The verifier computes
\[
    h^*(b_1, b_2) = (1{-}b_1)(1{-}b_2) e'_a + (1{-}b_1) b_2 e'_b + b_1(1{-}b_2) e'_c + b_1 b_2 e'_q,
\]
samples $(r_1, r_2) \randpick \extensionfield^2$, and reduces to the single claim $h(r_1, r_2, r, \beta, \gamma) = h^*(r_1, r_2)$. In this special exact hypercube covering case the prover needs send \emph{zero} field elements. Note that because SelectorMerge aligned all data coordinates, every coordinate the claims share is identical and the selector bits span the full hypercube, so this all-at-once reduction applies directly.

At this point, the $5$ evaluation claims on $5$ oracles have been consolidated into $2$ claims: $h(z_h) = v_h$ (indexed, dimension $2 + m + \mu + \nu$) and $\tilde{s}(\beta, \gamma) = e'_s$ (committed, dimension $\mu + \nu$).

\paragraph{Ring-switch packing.}
Each of the two evaluation claims are fed independently into the ring-switch packing PIOR of~\cite{EC:DiaPos25}, which exploits the tower $\field_p \subset \field_{p^2} \subset \cdots \subset \extensionfield$ (with $s = \log_2[\extensionfield:\field_p]$) to produce a claim on a packed polynomial of dimension $R - s$, where $R$ is the original dimension. The reduction is an $s$-round degree-$1$ sumcheck with round-$i$ challenges in $\field_{p^{2^i}}$; the prover sends $s$ field elements per polynomial. Applied to our two claims, packing produces $\hat{h}$ of dimension $R_h - s$ (where $R_h = 2 + m + \mu + \nu$) and $\hat{s}$ of dimension $R_s - s$ (where $R_s = \mu + \nu$). We use the tower construction for odd primes given in \cref{sec:odd-prime-tower}.

\parhead{Interleaved BaseFold opening}
Both packed claims are opened via BaseFold~\cite{basefold}, which interleaves an $\meq$-sumcheck with IOPP folding: at each round, the verifier sends one challenge, the prover folds the codeword and sends the next sumcheck round polynomial, and commits the folded codeword via a Merkle tree.

Since $\hat{h}$ has $R_h - R_s = 2 + m$ more variables than $\hat{s}$, the prover first folds $\hat{h}$ solo for $2 + m$ rounds, bringing its codeword to the same length as $\hat{s}$'s. For the remaining $R_s - s$ rounds, both polynomials fold with the same challenge: a single uniform $r_i \randpick \extensionfield$ serves both IOPP instances (soundness holds by a union bound). At each shared round, the prover builds one MMCS-style Merkle tree whose leaf~$j$ contains the pair $(\pi^{(h)}_i[j],\, \pi^{(s)}_i[j])$ of folded codeword values. The prover sends one tree root and two sumcheck round polynomials (one per evaluation target). During the query phase, a single authentication path per shared round opens both values simultaneously, and fold consistency is checked independently for each polynomial.  The saving is one full set of Merkle authentication paths over the shared rounds: $(R_s - s) \cdot t \cdot \lambda_{\mathrm{hash}}$ bits, where $t$ is the number of query repetitions.

\emph{Full PIOR chain.} Starting from $5$ evaluation claims on $5$ oracles:
\[
    \mathrm{SelectorMerge} \;\to\; \mathrm{MultiPoint} \;\to\; \mathrm{DP24\;Packing} \;\to\; \mathrm{BaseFold\;(solo\;+\;interleaved)}.
\]
The prover maintains two original Merkle commitments ($T_h$ from preprocessing, $T_s$ from the prover's first message). After packing, the prover commits $T_{\hat{h}}$ and $T_{\hat{s}}$. The solo-fold phase produces $2 + m$ folding commitments for $\hat{h}$; the interleaved phase produces $R_s - s$ shared commitments. The PIOP-level reductions cost $0$~prover field elements for MultiPoint (the four claims cover $\bits^2$) and $2s$~for packing ($s$ per polynomial).
